How We Use Data

A plain-language explanation of why StepGate needs certain permissions.

Last updated: 2026-04-15

Why StepGate needs app usage access

StepGate reads usage data only to track time in the apps you choose to control and detect when their limit is exhausted.

Why StepGate needs step/activity data

Step data helps StepGate adjust available access after new step records arrive. Data may appear with delay because sources and Health Connect sync over time.

Why accessibility is needed

Accessibility is used only to detect when a controlled app is opened and to show the limit screen when your limit is reached. It is not used to read messages, screen text, keyboard input, or app content.

Does my data leave the device?

No. StepGate stores this data locally on your device and does not send it to our servers.

What if I do not grant a permission?

If a permission is not granted, StepGate still runs, but some features are limited. For example, without step data we cannot update step-based access.

What StepGate does not access

  • Messages or chat contents
  • On-screen text in other apps
  • Keyboard input or passwords
  • Photos, files, or contacts
