How We Use Data

Plain-language explanation of why StepGate needs certain permissions.

Last updated: 2026-05-20

Why app usage access is needed

StepGate reads usage data only to detect the current foreground app, track time in selected controlled apps, and know when access is exhausted.

Why steps / activity are needed

StepGate can count steps with the phone sensor when Activity Recognition is granted.

Health Connect is optional. If you connect it, StepGate can use external step history; sync may still be delayed by source apps.

Why display over other apps is needed

StepGate uses display-over-apps permission only to show the limit screen over selected controlled apps. It does not read messages, screen text, keyboard input, or app content.

Why notifications and background work are needed

StepGate uses a foreground notification so protection remains visible while Android allows background monitoring. Battery/autostart settings can help the protection restart after reboot or continue in the background on some devices.

Does data leave the device?

No. StepGate stores this data locally and does not send usage data, selected app lists, steps, or Health Connect data to StepGate servers.

What if permission is not granted?

StepGate still opens, but related features are limited. Without Usage Access, it cannot measure selected app time. Without display-over-apps permission, it cannot show the blocking screen over selected apps. Without step data, step-based access updates are unavailable.

What StepGate does not access

  • Messages or chat contents
  • On-screen text in other apps
  • Keyboard input or passwords
  • Photos, files, or contacts
