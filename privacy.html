Privacy Policy

This policy explains how StepGate handles your data.

Last updated: 2026-04-16

Introduction

StepGate helps limit access to selected apps and restore access through movement. This policy explains what data is accessed and why.

Data accessed

  • App usage data for selected controlled apps.
  • Step/activity data from Health Connect and/or supported on-device sources.
  • Permission states required for app features.

Permissions used

  • Usage Access — to read usage for selected apps.
  • Accessibility Service — to detect launch of selected apps and show the limit screen.
  • Activity Recognition / Health Connect — to read step data.
  • Overlay permission — to show the limit screen over controlled apps (when enabled).

How data is used

  • Calculate baseline access from recent usage history.
  • Update daily access after new step data arrives.
  • Determine available time for controlled apps.
  • Show a limit screen when a controlled app reaches its daily limit.

Storage and sharing

StepGate stores data locally on your device and does not send usage/step data to our servers.

We do not sell or share personal data with third parties.

What StepGate does not read

  • Messages, chats, or email contents
  • On-screen text in other apps
  • Keyboard input or passwords
  • Photos, files, or contacts

Accessibility disclosure

Accessibility is used only to detect launch of selected controlled apps and show the limit screen after limit exhaustion.

StepGate does not use Accessibility to read messages, extract text, capture keystrokes, or perform unrelated automated actions.

Health Connect rationale

When Health Connect is enabled, StepGate requests read access to step data only. This is used only for step-based access logic.

Data retention and deletion

Data remains on device until app data is cleared or StepGate is uninstalled.
